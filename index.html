About me

Supermom in Training: The Real Story

They say that when a child is born, a mother is born too. I found this to be true the moment I held my baby girl for the first time. As I cried tears of joy, she stopped crying — proof that our bond was instant and unbreakable. Now, I might sound a bit ridiculous, but I never imagined I’d be the one giving her first bath or massage. For nine months, I devoured every baby care video and attended every possible class. I was preparing to be Supermom, complete with a cape and a diaper bag. But then reality hit. As days turned into a sleepless blur, everything spiralled out of control. I was a full-time international student with no family around to help. My husband was working double shifts, leaving me to juggle motherhood and my internship. I was exhausted, trying to live up to that perfect Instagram mom who reads bedtime stories, takes leisurely walks, and makes brushing kid’s teeth look fun. Life’s biggest lesson slapped me in the face: pretending to be strong isn’t the same as actually being strong. Everyone needs a break. Trying to do it all was like trying to eat a five-course meal in one bite — not going to end well. Living one day at a time helped me build real strength. I was born to be a mom, but losing myself in the process wasn’t the answer. I paid a steep price for my perfectionism. I had constant headaches, and my brain was on a never-ending to-do list. Communication with my husband dwindled, and our relationship, already shaky, began to crumble. One day, I was crying in front of my daughter, and she wiped my tears. That was my wake-up call. I realized I needed to prioritize myself. Happy mom, happy child — it was that simple. So, I started taking small steps — enjoying a peaceful cup of tea, savouring a glass of wine while binge-watching Netflix on weekends, doing Sudoku and crafts, taking evening walks with my daughter, and indulging in guilt-free desserts. Each bit of happiness I found for myself reflected in my daughter. In the end, being a mom isn’t about perfection. It’s about love, resilience, and finding joy in the chaos. And that, I believe, is the best lesson I’ve learned on this wild ride. Hey there, super moms and dads! If you enjoyed my story and want to hear more about the real, messy, and beautiful journey of parenthood, like, share and follow me. Let’s share our experiences, support each other, and laugh at the craziness together. Got your own funny or touching parenting story? Share it in the comments below or on my blog. Let’s build a community where we can all learn, grow, and find joy in the chaos!
